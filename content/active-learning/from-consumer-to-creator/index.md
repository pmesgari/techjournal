## The Problem

As an enthusiastic learner, I consume a significant number of blogs, articles, and white papers daily. But Iâ€™ve realized that skimming is not learning. Passive absorption doesn't create the neural links required to actually apply knowledge. It just leaves me with a brain full of noise and a browser full of open tabs.

## The Solution

To break this pattern, I am introducing a new section to Techjournal called Active Learning. Here, I collect the resources I have not just read, but analyzed.

## The Framework

For every resource listed here, I follow a three-step cycle:

### Input
What I read/watched and the core concepts.

### Application
A concrete plan on how I will implement this in my current projects or workflow.

### Reflection
A retrospective update on whether it actually worked.

This journal is my way of practicing clarity, cementing my own knowledge, and building a public repository of things that actually matter.
